Ju Zhang discusses her, "A Contextualist-Reliabilist Justification Theory of Moral Beliefs." Thursday, 11/12 at 12:30 pm in WAG 316.

ABSTRACT:
I will try to construct a contextualist- reliablist theory of justification of moral beliefs by arguing that (1) in ordinary context, at least some of our general moral beliefs (beliefs constituting moral principles) are contextually basic, and consequently they are in no need of justification and can be an epistemic basis for justifying other moral beliefs. Moreover, these contextually basic moral beliefs can legitimately terminate the regress of justification of moral beliefs. This is the second response to moral skepticism; (2) moral perception is not only possible but also can be a reliable moral belief-forming process in ordinary context given those contextually basic moral beliefs; (3) at least some of our perceptual moral beliefs (usually beliefs constituting particular moral judgments) can therefore be justified.
