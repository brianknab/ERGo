UPCOMING:

Brian Knab discusses Chapter 3 of Jeffreys' Theory of Probability. Thursday, 9/3 at 12:30 pm in WAG 316.

ABSTRACT: Confronted with Bertrand-style paradoxes -- like van Fraassen's cube factory puzzle -- Jeffreys writes, "a succession of authors have said that the prior probability is nonsense and therefore that [Bayesianism], which cannot work without it, is nonsense too." However, he continues, the solution to this problem "is in fact very easy."

I'll try to make sense of Jeffreys' "very easy" solution, and will question whether or not it's tenable.
